Introduction

These so-called bleak times are necessary to go through in order to get to a much, much better place.”

― David Lynch

In March of 2020, COVID-19 appeared to arrive everywhere at once and upended our lives and the world. The outbreak of Covid-19 impacted and altered the way people worked and lived as well as our bondings and our perception of the world. College students, the ambitiously energetic, sensitive yet vulnerable young generation, faced and dealt with challenges from both physical and mental health on their passage to adulthood. Students who choose to study abroad had to make choices during this pandemic as being apart from their families; whether reunion with their loved one from the other side of the globe or staying in the U.S., we all have to adjust to the changes in lifestyle, cope with the emotional rollercoaster, and find peace in dilemmas. 

This digital storytelling project presents the narratives of two UCLA students, Jiayun (Judy) He and Zhengbai (Jessica) Xia. More than one year later, we look back at this chaotic year and see how we and our lives have changed by reflecting from the memory pieces. It compares and contrasts the difficulties we faced and our emotional states at different stages of the pandemic. Judy went back to China at the end of March in 2020; Jessica chose to stay in Los Angeles; however, we both adapted new lifestyles and discovered some new understanding of the world, our life and ourselves.
